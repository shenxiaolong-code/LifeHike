**15 Years of Trekking Evolution:**
I started fully geared but now rely almost entirely on my 65L pack. I’ve discarded poles (keep them stowed), knee braces, and wrist guards.

**The Knee Paradox:**
15 years ago, I descended Zhangjiajie empty-handed and ended up limping. 
Today, I can jog down trails or carry a heavy pack with no poles, experiencing zero discomfort.

**My Conclusion:** Heavy-load trekking, combined with proper biomechanics, does NOT damage your knees or meniscus—it **strengthens** them. The body is a self-optimizing system that thrives under calculated stress.
*Note: After the "Mystery Box" challenge on Laojun Mountain, I’ve added crampons to my gear for handling extreme terrains.*
